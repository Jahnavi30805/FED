James Webb Space Telescope

The James Webb Space Telescope (JWST) is the most powerful space telescope ever built, launched on December 25, 2021, by NASA in collaboration with the European Space Agency (ESA) and the Canadian Space Agency (CSA). It serves as the scientific successor to the Hubble Space Telescope, designed to explore the universe in unprecedented detail.

Key Features

  • Unprecedented infrared sensitivity
  • Large primary mirror
  • Four scientific instruments
  • Exploring early galaxies
  • Studying exoplanet atmospheres
